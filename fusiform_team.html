Our Advisors

The people that keep us out of trouble.



  • David Wise
    CEO of Pharos Biologicals

    David W. Wise has been deeply involved in the medtech innovation and startup community in Baltimore for a number of years, principally as the Venture Advisor on the direct investment program by the Abell Foundation. In that capacity he has been involved in two Series A rounds and one Series B round of investments, as well as five seed stage investments and three loans. He is a member of the Advisory Board of Betamore, a technology incubator. He also served on investment review committees for Accelerate Baltimore, bwtech, the VOLT Fund and The Fischell Department of Bioengineering at the University of Maryland in College Park. He is a member of the Association for Corporate Growth and sits on the Bioscience subcommittee of the Greater Baltimore Committee. From 2005 to 2014 he was the President and Chief Executive Officer of The Genetics and IVF Institute (GIVF) in Fairfax, Virginia, an international women’s healthcare company with operations in China. Altogether he has almost sixteen years of C-Level experience having previously been Chief Operating Officer of the Lang Companies in Delafield, Wisconsin which sold the company to a private equity firm, and as Chief Financial and Administrative Officer for the Centennial Companies in Tysons Corner, Virginia where he had been brought in to manage a workout of the company’s $450 million in debt. He was also the Vice President for Corporate Development at BDM International, a Fortune 1000 company in which the Carlyle Group was the principal investor. At BDM he participated on the IPO team, completed numerous acquisitions including an international transaction, and co-managed the sale of the company to TRW in a $1 billion transaction. Mr. Wise serves on the board of the Maryland Humanities Council and the advisory board of Lawrenceville Plasma Physics. He is a member of the US Navy Strategy Discussion Group and of the International Institute of Strategic Studies (London). He graduated with highest honors from the University of Dayton in 1975 and in 1982 received an MALD degree in international business from The Fletcher School of Law & Diplomacy, which is co-administered by Tufts and Harvard Universities.

  • Peter Raymond
    CEO of Human Condition

    Peter E. Raymond is our Development Strategy Advisor. Peter is an innovator, serial entrepreneur, investor, and creative strategist. By merging art, science, and business acumen, he develops industry changing companies, models and emerging tech. He is looking to connect with other doers and organizations who can accelerate disruptive yet socially conscious solutions for the world's toughest challenges without fear. Peter has built Human Condition to execute at scale across education, environment, construction, renewable energy, healthcare, technology, sports, media, agriculture, transportation, bigdata, and finance sectors. His work has been featured multiple times by New York Times, Boston Globe, Wired, Popular Science, PBS, Apple, New Scientist, Mashable, and he has appeared on ABC and CBS Nightly News. He and his teams uncover unique insights and develop innovation strategies that result in socially conscious solutions, brands, products, revenue models, education, technology, and simulations for Fortune 500's, startups, nonprofits, governments, and their customers, such as: The Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, The City of New York, Nissan, Hearst Publishing, Viacom, ESPN, IKEA, Tom Ford, Astra­Zeneca, Bristol­Myers Squibb, Biogen Idec, Boehringer Ingelheim, Nokia, NAACP, Africa America Institute, Drum Major Institute, and The Baseball Hall of Fame. As a Senior Fellow at Thomas Jefferson School of Population Health his focus was empathy and behavioral change, as well as developing the next generation of simulation and safety wearable technology to enable medical adoption.

  • Mark Hopkins
    CEO, President Dankmeyer Prosthetics & Orthotics

    Mark is a Certified Prosthetist Orthotist and a Licensed Physical Therapist, who has been with Dankmeyer since 1992. Originally from Newark, Delaware, Mark has a BS with a Certificate in Physical Therapy (PT) from the University of Delaware. It was during his internships there that he had several experiences with amputees. Later, when he began working in PT at Johns Hopkins, he started covering the Amputee Clinic. After meeting Charles Dankmeyer, he decided to go into the prosthetics program at Northwestern University Medical School Prosthetics and Orthotics Center, and earned a Certificate in Prosthetics there. Eventually he left Hopkins to come to work full time at Dankmeyer, Inc. where he became Clinic Director. During that time he completed his MBA at The Johns Hopkins University. He is currently adjunct faculty at the University of Maryland School of Medicine School of Physical Therapy and The Johns Hopkins University School of Medicine, Department of Physical Medicine and Rehabilitation. A number of our patients know that Mark is a runner, who has completed several marathons.

  • Michael Abbruzzese
    Legal Counsel

    Along with Fusiform, Michael Abbruzzese is Legal Counsel for Kaspersky Lab and is responsible for all legal operations of Kaspersky entities in the Americas region. As part of the Kaspersky worldwide legal and security team, Michael has worked with customers, partners and vendors dealing with Kaspersky subsidiaries in the United States, Brazil and Mexico while also protecting Kaspersky’s intellectual property rights within these regions. Michael brings almost 20 years of experience in technology contracts negotiation and partner management in the technology industry. Prior to joining Kaspersky Lab in 2012 and Fusiform in 2016, Michael served in various legal positions for Netezza Corporation and Guardium, Inc. (both acquired by IBM) and RSA Security.
